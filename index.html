Featured Research

A few areas of my current work.

SARLink

Exploring satellite backscatter connectivity using synthetic aperture radar signals.

Thermal Noise Backscatter

Investigating communication and sensing systems that operate through thermal noise and extreme power constraints.

Low-Power Wireless Sensing

Designing wireless sensing systems that do more with less by using ambient signals and efficient hardware.

About

My research sits at the intersection of wireless communication, sensing, electromagnetics, and low-power systems. I am especially interested in how ambient signals can enable new sensing and communication capabilities under tight resource constraints.

Geneva Ecola

PhD Candidate in Electrical Engineering, Stanford University

I am a PhD candidate in electrical engineering at Stanford interested in ultra-low-power sensing and communication systems using ambient radio frequency signals.

Email LinkedIn Google Scholar GitHub

About

I work on wireless systems for low-power sensing and communication. My research explores how ambient signals can be used to enable new sensing and communication capabilities under extreme power constraints.

PhD Advisor: Zerina Kapetanovic

Current Research

My current work includes communication and sensing using thermal noise, as well as backscatter communication using synthetic aperture radar satellites.

  • Geneva Ecola, Bill Yen, Ana Banzer Morgado, Bodhi Priyantha, Ranveer Chandra, and Zerina Kapetanovic, “SARLink: Satellite Backscatter Connectivity using Synthetic Aperture Radar,” in SenSys ’25, 2025.
